Warning signs (5xx)
The importance of a warning sign is emphasised by the red border around its edge and the triangular shape. Some warning signs do not have a red border like sharp deviation.

B: Priority signs, C: Prohibitory or restrictive signs, D: Mandatory signs

Regulatory signs (6xx)
Signs in circular red borders are prohibitive, whether or not they also have a diagonal red line. Circular blue signs mainly give a positive (mandatory) instruction.

E: Special regulation signs, F: Information, facilities, or service signs

Information signs (8xx)
Informational signs are usually rectangular and generally blue with white text.

G: Direction, position, or indication sign(Tourist Destinations)

Tourist Destinations (Txx)
The term "directional sign" covers both Advance Direction Signs (ADS), placed on the approach to a junction, and Direction Signs (DS) at the junction itself, showing where to turn. A DS has a chevron (pointed) end, and this type is also referred to as a flag-type sign.
